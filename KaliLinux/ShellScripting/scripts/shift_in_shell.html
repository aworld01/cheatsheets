Shift statement

Shift is a bash built-in which kind of removes arguments from the beginning of the argument list. Given that the 3 arguments provided to the script are available in $1 $2 $3, then a call to shift will make $2 the new $1. A shift by two making new $1 the old $3. Bydefault n=1 in shift.

#!/bin/bash echo $1 $2 $3 $4 $5 shift echo $1 $2 $3 $4 $5 shift 2 echo $1 $2 $3 $4 $5 # type: ./test.sh one two three four five

#!/bin/bash num=0 until [ $num -gt 10 ] do echo $num ((num++)) done

#!/bin/bash num=0 until [ $num -gt 10 ] do echo $num ((++num)) done

#!/bin/bash num=0 until (( $num > 10 )) do echo $num ((++num)) done
